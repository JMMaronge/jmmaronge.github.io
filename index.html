Hi, I’m Jake!

Note: I am currently job searching (mostly post-docs), and I would be very interested to discuss any opportunities!

I am a 5th year PhD candidate in the Department of Statistics at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. I am working with Paul Rathouz and Jon Schildcrout on generalizing the notion of case-control studies to non-binary responses, which we refer to as generalized case-control studies. I am interested in developing a flexible, comprehensive framework for the analysis of these studies for a wide-variety of response types. In addition, I aim to give guidelines for practitioners for the efficient design of these studies. Also, I am very excited to be a Morse Scholar. My Morse Society Webpage can be seen here.

I received my MS in Biostatistics from the Louisiana State University Health Sciences Center School of Public Health, where I worked with Zhide Fang on optimal designs of experiments.

Previously, I spent a summer at the Johns Hopkins University Bloomberg School of Public Health working with Ciprian Crainiceanu, John Muschelli, and Elizabeth Sweeney on lesion segmentation in neuroimaging. During this time, I developed my first R package, medals, which stands for Memory Efficient Decomposition for Analysis of Local neighborhood moments for Segmentation.
